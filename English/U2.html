Examples vs Evidence — Interactive Lesson

Objective: review fact vs opinion. Learn the difference between examples and evidence. Practise using evidence to support facts.

1) Introduction

Fact: “Dogs are mammals.”

Opinion: “Dogs are the best pets.”

Key point: facts can be proven. Opinions are beliefs.

Hook — the missing evidence

Claim: “Recycling is important. For example, many people recycle.”

Question: What’s missing?

Guide: Example shows; Evidence proves.

2) Presentation

Definitions

TypePurposeGuiding question
Example Shows what you mean “Like what?”
Evidence Proves your point “How do you know?”
Model examples
  • Example: “My friend recycles bottles.”
  • Evidence: “Studies show recycling cuts landfill waste by 35%.”

Pairings

ExampleEvidence
My school has a garden Studies show gardens improve nutrition by 20%
Some people ride bikes City data shows bike commuting reduces traffic by 15%
Animals live in rainforests Scientists have identified millions of species there
Think–Pair–Share: “Exercise is good for children.”
Write one example and one evidence.

Partner task

Topic: Healthy eating. A writes an example. B writes evidence.

4) Production — Write a paragraph

Choose a topic and complete the frame. The checker verifies structure, not truth.

Checklist
  • Topic sentence present
  • One example using “For example”
  • One evidence using “Research shows”
  • Concluding sentence using “Therefore”
Sentence frames

Topic sentence: [Your point]
For example, [show it].
Research shows [proof or data].
Therefore, [sum up].

5) Summary

  • Example shows. Evidence proves.
  • Use both to make writing engaging and convincing.
  • Next: write strong expository paragraphs.
