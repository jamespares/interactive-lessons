8) Writing Practice: Personal Expository Paragraph

Prompt: Explain about a tradition your family celebrates. Remember to:

  • Use personal pronouns (I, we, my, our)
  • State only facts (no "I think", no "should")
  • End with a summary, not a conclusion
Model paragraph

"In my family, we celebrate Chinese New Year every January or February. My parents clean the house thoroughly before the festival begins. We decorate our home with red lanterns and couplets. On New Year's Eve, my whole family gathers for a large dinner. We eat dumplings, fish, and other traditional foods. Children receive red envelopes with money from elders. The celebration lasts for fifteen days. In summary, Chinese New Year is our most important family tradition."

9) Writing Practice: Impersonal Expository Paragraph

Prompt: Explain how plants make food (photosynthesis). Remember to:

  • Use impersonal language (it, they, plants)
  • State only facts
  • End with a summary
Model paragraph

"Plants make food through a process called photosynthesis. Chlorophyll in the leaves absorbs sunlight. The plant takes in carbon dioxide from the air through tiny pores. Water is absorbed by the roots from the soil. The plant combines these elements to produce glucose, a type of sugar. Oxygen is released as a byproduct. This process occurs mainly in the leaves. In summary, photosynthesis allows plants to convert light energy into chemical energy for growth."

11) Lesson Summary

Key Takeaways:

  • Expository writing is purely factual â€” no opinions!
  • Use personal expository (I, we, my) when writing about YOUR culture or YOUR experiences
  • Use impersonal expository (it, they, the) for general facts and scientific explanations
  • Expository writing ends with a summary, not a conclusion
  • Avoid opinion words: "I think", "should", "best", "worst", emotional language
  • Stick to facts that can be verified or proven
